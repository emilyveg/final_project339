Ways to Volunteer & Help Support DMUM!

(Hover to Learn More)

Creative Coping provides the opportunity for participants to explore coping strategies through expressive and experiential art, crafts, and activity-based games, like journaling and creating dream catchers. This therapy occurs in both an in-person and virtual format for participants and volunteers.

Sophie's Place is a dedicated music therapy environment in C.S. Mott Children's hospital to promote creativity, safety, and expression for patients and their families during their healing. Sophie's Place offers opportunities to record music, play a variety of instruments, and engage with music therapy sessions.

The Patient Technology Department at C.S. Mott Children's Hospital enhances patient experience through therapeutic video games and other technology platforms. This team offers in-room gaming, volunteer gamers and game developers, lego and robotics, and virtual reality experiences for patients!

One of the most unique therapies and volunteer opportunities DMUM funds is Adaptive Tree Climbing. This form of therapy allows patients with physical limitations to learn how to climb trees with the assistance of adaptive equipment.
